About Dr. Turner

A Leading Voice in African American Appalachian Studies

Dr. William H. Turner stands as one of the foremost authorities on Black life and culture in the Appalachian South. With decades of dedicated research, teaching, and community engagement, he has illuminated the often-overlooked histories and contributions of African Americans in this region.

William R. Kenan, Jr. Professor at Berea College

Dr. Turner currently holds the prestigious position of William R. Kenan, Jr. Professor at Berea College for the 2024-2026 term. This distinguished appointment, funded by the William R. Kenan, Jr. Charitable Trust, recognizes faculty who demonstrate exceptional excellence in both teaching and scholarly research.

Through this position, Dr. Turner continues to mentor the next generation of scholars while advancing critical research on the intersection of race, place, and identity in Appalachia.

Academic Background

Dr. Turner earned his Ph.D. in Sociology from the University of Notre Dame. His groundbreaking dissertation on African American communities in Appalachia laid the foundation for his lifelong commitment to documenting and preserving these important cultural histories.

Career Highlights

  • Distinguished Professor and Academic Administrator at multiple institutions
  • Research Associate at the University of Kentucky Appalachian Center
  • Consultant for the Appalachian Regional Commission
  • Vice President for University Engagement at Prairie View A&M University
  • Interim President of Kentucky State University

Publications & Book Sales

The Harlan Renaissance

Dr. Turner's landmark work chronicles the vibrant Black communities that flourished in Harlan County, Kentucky during the coal mining boom of the 1920s and 1930s. Through meticulous research and personal narratives, he reveals a previously untold chapter of Appalachian history.

Services

Keynote Speaking

Dr. Turner delivers compelling, insightful presentations on African American Appalachian history, cultural preservation, and the intersection of race and place. His talks are suitable for academic conferences, community events, corporate diversity initiatives, and educational institutions.

Historical Consultation

With decades of research experience, Dr. Turner provides expert consultation for documentary filmmakers, museum curators, historical societies, and authors working on projects related to Black Appalachian history and culture. His deep knowledge ensures historical accuracy and nuanced representation.

Academic Workshops

Dr. Turner conducts specialized workshops for faculty, graduate students, and researchers on methodologies for studying marginalized communities, oral history collection, archival research, and community-engaged scholarship. These interactive sessions provide practical skills and theoretical frameworks.

Media Interviews

Dr. Turner is available for interviews with journalists, podcasters, and media outlets seeking expert commentary on topics related to Appalachian studies, African American history, rural development, and contemporary issues facing Black communities in rural America.
